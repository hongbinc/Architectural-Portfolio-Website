City Buffer

Design Theme

    The design, presented to ACSA competition, offered a solution to urban life, by encouraging people to exercise after work, and an innovative steel design.

    The design proposes a center for swimming and bowling in Buffalo. It has been designed for a site in the heart of the city that is connected to Main Street by Market Arcade, and is alongside office towers, hotels and apartment bulidings.

    The center, designed for people who live and worked in the city, will provide them with bowling lanes and swimming pools. Both of these uses required large open spaces as one of criteria in the competition. The swimming pools are ideally located on the ground with other landscape water features, consequently the bowling lanes have been located on the upper floor within a structure that has been designed to act as a series of bridges. Both of the structure and skin of this buliding have been inspired by nature, the spine and butterfly scales.

Liquid Light

Design Theme

    The project proposes a scheme that includes an urban living accommodation, with public program that strategically activates and enriches the city.

    The urban strategy is shaped by natural light at the times of the day when people are most active. This light organized the scheme into eight "grounded" towers of dwelling and two "hanging towers" of gardens, all of which are linked by glazed corridors on three levels only. This allows the natural sunlight to pierce through the site to Allen street, which is an active pedestrian street. The towers ambiguous in scale that registering both as large monoliths and as miniature city. Folded brushed stainless steel cladding, which is animated by an ever-changing play of light, has a changing density of folds depending on what type of room is in the interior:bathrooms and bedrooms have more dense folds, while kitchens and living spaces have a more open rhythm.
